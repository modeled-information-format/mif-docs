# Remote Patient Monitoring for Heart Failure: A Systematic Review

<!-- ANTIPATTERN: titled as a systematic review, but there is no PRISMA flow
     diagram anywhere below — the mandatory element this genre exists to
     require is simply missing. A study count is asserted with no reconciled
     stage-by-stage record of how it was reached. -->

We reviewed the literature on remote patient monitoring for heart failure and
found 9 studies that met our criteria.

## Method

We searched PubMed and screened the results for relevance.

<!-- ANTIPATTERN: "Method" collapses eligibility criteria, information
     sources, search strategy, selection process, data items, and
     risk-of-bias assessment into one summary sentence — none of the six
     required Methods sub-elements is actually present. -->

## Results

Overall, remote monitoring reduced hospital readmissions across the included
studies.

<!-- ANTIPATTERN: "the included studies" names no PRISMA flow diagram, no
     stage counts (identified/screened/excluded/included), and no
     risk-of-bias table — the reader cannot audit how 9 studies were reached
     from however many records were originally identified, nor which were
     excluded or why. -->

## Conclusion

Remote monitoring works and should be adopted.

<!-- ANTIPATTERN: no Discussion section with limitations, no Registration &
     Protocol statement, and no References list — every claim above is an
     orphan fact with nothing to cite. -->
