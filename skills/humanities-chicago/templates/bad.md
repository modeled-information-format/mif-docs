## Introduction

Many modern novels use narrators whose account of events cannot be fully
trusted. This essay argues that unreliable narration is one of the defining
techniques of twentieth-century fiction.

## The Unreliable Narrator as Technique

Critics have long noted that narrators who distort or withhold information
force readers to reconstruct the "real" story themselves. This happens in
many famous novels and is widely discussed in the scholarship on narrative
theory.

## Conclusion

Unreliable narration remains a central technique because it lets authors
create irony and depth without stating things directly.
