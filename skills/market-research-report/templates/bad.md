# Market Research Report: Smart Water-Bottle Adoption

<!-- ANTIPATTERN: titled and structured as a market research report, but there
     is no Methodology section anywhere below — the mandatory sampling and
     fieldwork disclosure this genre exists to require is simply missing. -->

## Background & Objectives

Our client wants to know whether consumers are interested in smart,
app-connected water bottles before greenlighting the product line.

## Findings

62% of consumers say they would buy a smart water bottle if the price were
right, and hydration-tracking is the most requested feature.

<!-- ANTIPATTERN: a specific, quantified claim (62%) is presented as evidence
     with no disclosed sample frame, method, size, or fieldwork basis anywhere
     in the report — the reader has no way to judge whether this number came
     from a rigorous stratified panel or a hallway poll of ten people. -->

## Conclusions & Recommendations

We recommend greenlighting the smart water-bottle line and leading with
hydration-tracking in marketing.

     all. -->
